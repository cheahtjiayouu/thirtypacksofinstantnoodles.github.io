Vanilla Ice cream-Rm12.50

A silken quenelle of hand-churned vanilla ice cream, crafted from double cream sourced from grass-fed Normandy cows, infused gently with the seeds of Madagascar Bourbon vanilla pods—sun-cured and split mere hours before infusion. The custard base is cooked sous-vide to preserve the subtle floral notes and prevent over-emulsification, resulting in a texture that is simultaneously rich, airy, and velvety.

Chocolate Ice cream-Rm 13.10

This artisanal ice cream is a study in intensity and refinement—crafted from 72% single-origin Ecuadorian dark chocolate, delicately tempered into a custard base of organic egg yolks and cream, slow-cooked to achieve a custard with the silken consistency of velvet. On the nose, a bouquet of roasted cacao, espresso, and faint black cherry rises. The palate opens with a bold bitterness, quickly enveloped by layers of deep cocoa, toasted hazelnut, and a trace of molasses. A hint of sea salt draws out the complexity of the chocolate, while a whisper of vanilla on the finish elevates and rounds the profile. Texturally indulgent, almost truffle-like in density, yet melting into silk upon the tongue,Served in its purest form.

Strawberry ice cream-Rm12.70

This seasonal creation captures the fleeting essence of peak-summer strawberries—handpicked at dawn from organic heirloom cultivars grown in volcanic soil. The berries are gently macerated with raw cane sugar to awaken their natural acidity, then folded into a crème anglaise base enriched with farm-fresh cream and a touch of buttermilk for brightness. The result is a delicate pastel pink with no artificial hue—just the honest blush of ripe fruit. On the palate, notes of wild strawberry, hibiscus, and a hint of balsamic unfold in perfect harmony, balanced by a rounded, creamy finish. Each spoonful recalls the texture of a just-bitten berry: yielding, juicy, ephemeral. Served simply, without garnish.

Cheesecake Rm 21.70

An aerated cheesecake crafted from single-origin Madagascar vanilla and artisanal cream cheese, gently baked in a bain-marie to achieve an impossibly smooth, cloud-like texture. The crust—a delicate sablé Breton infused with toasted almond and brown butter—offers a subtle crunch, grounding the dish with its nutty warmth. Served barely chilled to enhance the natural richness of the dairy, each bite reveals layers of restrained sweetness, floral vanilla, and a whisper of citrus zest. Accompanied by a brushstroke of Sicilian lemon gel and a quenelle of cultured crème fraîche, the dish is finished with a snow of freeze-dried raspberries to evoke contrast in both texture and acidity.

Chocolate cake-Rm 22.90

This rich yet refined cake is composed of 75% single-origin Venezuelan dark chocolate, folded into a velvety emulsion of farm-fresh eggs and cultured butter—baked just to the edge of set, where the interior remains molten and mousse-like. The absence of flour allows the purity of the chocolate to speak in full depth: earthy, bitter, with notes of espresso, tobacco leaf, and black cherry. A whisper of sea salt heightens the profile, while a dusting of raw cocoa powder adds a final, bittersweet kiss. Served warm, it rests on a smear of smoked crème anglaise and is paired with a quenelle of sour cherry sorbet, providing a sharp contrast to the cake’s decadence.
